Communications

Communications is an area of computer science based around how computers share information via networks.

The Internet

The largest computer network is the internet, which connects computers all over the world. This allows people to find information they might not otherwise have access to, and also to communicate with people all over the world.As well as spreading knowledge, the internet can give minorities a voice, and can also be a platform for charitable projects since content is seen by people all over the world. An example of this is the save bletchley park project, which gained momentum via the use of twitter.

Mobile communication

Moblie devices, such as phones, have many advantages. For example:

  • Emergencies - mobile devices can be used to call the emergency services quickly, potentially saving lives
  • Businesses - employees can stay in contact all the time, and may also be saved long commutes if their work can be done online
  • Family - mobile devices allow families to stay in contact even when they are not in the same place, improving relationships

Distributed Computing

Distributed computingis used to solve problems that would otherwise require a supercomputer, often for projects which have difficulty finding funding. Tasks are split between multiple computers in different places, so they can run in parallel as if a multi-core processor was being used. This means that the program can be run on volunteers' computers, usually while they are not being used, and can be used to solve extremely difficult problems. An example of this is SETI.
